# AI-mediated teamwork topic assessment

Use this reference when ingesting sources about AI as a teammate, AI in teams, cross-functional collaboration, team performance, expertise integration, or social/motivational functions of GenAI at work.

## Pattern from Dell'Acqua et al. (2025), *The Cybernetic Teammate*

The central contribution was not generic human-AI collaboration. It was AI's effect on the **team as the work-design unit**: whether GenAI can reproduce some benefits that normally justify human teaming.

Promote or update `[[ai-mediated-teamwork]]` when a source directly addresses one or more of these team functions:

- **Performance pooling:** AI-supported individuals or teams matching or exceeding human-team performance.
- **Expertise integration:** AI helping workers reason across functional/professional boundaries that teams normally bridge.
- **Coordination and team architecture:** evidence about whether work should be done by a human team, a solo worker with AI, a human-AI pair, or a differently composed team.
- **Social/motivational texture:** AI changing positive/negative affect, perceived social support, relatedness, team climate, or motivation in teamwork-like contexts.
- **Team-function substitution:** AI substituting for specific team benefits before any job-level labor substitution occurs.

## Do not flatten into broad collaboration

If the source uses “teammate” language but the actual evidence is about responsibility, oversight, advice-following, delegation, or authority, update `[[human-ai-collaboration]]`, `[[agentic-delegation]]`, or `[[human-ai-task-allocation]]` instead.

If the source shows AI changing whether teams need to be formed or how cross-functional expertise is supplied, create/update `[[ai-mediated-teamwork]]` and connect it outward to:

- `[[human-ai-collaboration]]` for accountability and stewardship caveats.
- `[[work-redesign]]` for team architecture and role/workflow implications.
- `[[automation-and-substitution]]` for team-function substitution versus labor-demand claims.
- `[[ai-mediated-work-experience]]` for affective, motivational, and social texture.
- `[[human-ai-task-taxonomy]]` when task-level details determine whether the team evidence generalizes.

## Completion-note language

When proposing this topic, name the distinction plainly: “This is not just collaboration. The source is about whether AI reproduces the performance, expertise-sharing, and social/motivational functions that make teams valuable.”

## Evidence caveat

Short-run experimental evidence that AI-supported individuals match non-AI teams should not be converted into job-level or team-elimination claims. Surface limits around coordination, conflict resolution, mentoring, collective commitment, implementation, accountability, and durable trust.
