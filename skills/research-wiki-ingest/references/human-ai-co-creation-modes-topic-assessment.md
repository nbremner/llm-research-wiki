# Human-AI co-creation modes topic assessment

Use this reference during research-wiki ingests when a source's central contribution is not merely that humans and AI collaborate, but that people enact distinct workflow modes for steering GenAI-supported work over time.

## Promotion signal

Promote or update `[[human-ai-co-creation-modes]]` when a source distinguishes recurring patterns of agency across an AI-enabled workflow, especially along questions like:

- who selects what needs to be done;
- who identifies how it gets done;
- whether the human remains cognitively engaged in framing, method selection, evaluation, or iteration;
- whether the mode produces different skilling, calibration, accuracy, accountability, or oversight outcomes.

Do not flatten these sources into `[[human-ai-collaboration]]` by default. Collaboration is the umbrella; co-creation modes are the workflow-pattern substructure.

## Canonical seed example

`[[2025-randazzo-cyborgs-centaurs-self-automators]]` identifies three GenAI knowledge-work modes in a BCG consultant field experiment:

- **Fused Co-Creation / Cyborg** — GenAI integrated throughout the workflow; associated with GenAI-related newskilling while maintaining task expertise.
- **Directed Co-Creation / Centaur** — human preserves control over analytical logic while using GenAI selectively; associated with task/domain upskilling and highest recommendation accuracy in the study.
- **Abdicated Co-Creation / Self-Automator** — human delegates much of problem solving/evaluation to GenAI; associated with neither task nor AI expertise development during the episode.

The key construct is mode-specific work architecture, not user attitude toward AI or simple adoption.

## Topic-map pattern

When this signal appears:

1. **Create/update `[[human-ai-co-creation-modes]]`** if the source contributes a typology, mechanism, or evidence about mode-specific outcomes.
2. **Update `[[human-ai-collaboration]]` only to position the mode construct** relative to the broader collaboration category; avoid re-summarizing the entire source there.
3. **Update `[[work-redesign]]`** when the source implies design choices about who steers, executes, evaluates, learns, or remains accountable across the workflow.
4. **Update `[[ai-mediated-learning]]` and/or `[[ai-induced-skill-erosion]]`** when mode differences affect newskilling, upskilling, bypass, cognitive debt, or oversight capacity.
5. **Update `[[overview]]`** if the mode construct is promoted into the topic map or becomes a recurring open question.

## Pitfalls

- Do not treat Cyborg/Centaur/Self-Automator labels as stable worker types unless the evidence supports trait-like stability. In most sources, they are enacted modes or workflows.
- Do not assume one mode dominates on all outcomes. A source may imply tradeoffs among immediate accuracy, AI fluency, domain upskilling, efficiency, accountability, and future oversight capacity.
- Abdication is not automatically harmful. It may be efficient for routine, low-risk, or clearly inside-frontier tasks; the risk is harmful bypass when the task is also supposed to preserve judgment or expertise.
- Check for existing `[[human-ai-co-creation-modes]]` links before adding connection bullets. Merge with existing bullets rather than duplicating links with slightly different wording.
- If a new topic is proposed after the source record has already been committed, add the new source `## Feeds` link only in the owner-approved synthesis proposal, not in the source auto-commit.
