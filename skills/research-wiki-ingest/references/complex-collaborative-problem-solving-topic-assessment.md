# Complex/collaborative problem solving topic assessment

Use this pattern when ingesting sources about CPS/ColPS, 21st-century skills, transversal skills, microworld assessment, nonroutine problem solving, collaborative problem solving, or future-skill assessment in I-O psychology.

## Topic-map decision

Do not flatten CPS/ColPS into generic `competency-modeling`, `job-analysis`, or `construct-validity` if the source's central contribution is about dynamic problem interaction and assessment design. A precise topic such as `complex-collaborative-problem-solving` is warranted when the source treats CPS/ColPS as bounded constructs with problem features, active knowledge acquisition/application, collaboration demands, and/or computer-based microworld assessment.

## Natural integrations

- `job-analysis`: CPS/ColPS show the limits of static current-task descriptions when work becomes nonroutine, dynamic, and collaborative; use them to sharpen what job analysis must represent before assessment can be job-relevant for changing work.
- `competency-modeling`: CPS/ColPS are a safer path through broad “future skills” rhetoric because they translate capability language into observable problem-solving behavior and assessment tasks.
- `construct-validity`: CPS/ColPS are a positive-but-unfinished construct-validity case: more bounded than generic future-skills labels, but still needing evidence that microworld performance supports workplace inferences.
- `human-capital-resource-measurement`: CPS/ColPS can be candidate inputs to human-capital capability claims, but individual or microworld scores do not automatically aggregate into unit-level HCR or organizational advantage.
- `work-redesign`: AI-mediated work may shift human contribution toward problem framing, exception handling, coordination, verification, and adaptive control; connect only when the source supports this redesign claim.

## Pitfalls

- Do not let local Drive triage labels such as “Non-Academic” override DOI/Crossref or the article’s actual publication status.
- Do not overclaim direct organizational validation when the source mainly imports evidence from educational assessment or large-scale testing; surface the transfer/simulation-validity question explicitly.
- Do not treat every “21st-century skills” source as a new topic. Promote only when the source gives bounded constructs or assessment logic; otherwise integrate into competency modeling or defer as a watchlist candidate.
