# Human capital resource measurement topic-assessment note

Use this pattern when ingesting sources about human capital resources (HCR), workforce capability, skills capital, AI capital, or measures that try to turn human/AI capability into organizational performance evidence.

## Signal from Zhang et al. (2023)

`2023-zhang-human-capital-resources` warranted a precise new topic, `human-capital-resource-measurement`, rather than being flattened into `construct-validity`, `competency-modeling`, or `ai-workforce-impact-measurement`.

Core reusable contribution:

- HCR is a collective unit- or firm-level resource emerging from employees' KSAOs and compound KSAOs that are relevant to valued organizational outcomes.
- HCR measures can be **deficient** when they omit parts of the construct domain.
- HCR measures can be **contaminated** when they include antecedents, practices, attitudes, expenses, outcomes, or other non-HCR constructs.
- Prediction and explanation can diverge: partially contaminated HCR measures may predict performance more strongly because they include adjacent performance-relevant constructs, while becoming weaker evidence about HCR itself.

## Topic-map rule

Create or update `human-capital-resource-measurement` when the source is centrally about measuring collective human capability, HCR, skills capital, workforce capital, or capability indices. Do not use `construct-validity` as the only home unless the source is a generic measurement-validity paper with no durable HCR/workforce-capability construct.

Use adjacent pages as links, not substitutes:

- `construct-validity` — construct deficiency/contamination, prediction-vs-explanation, nomological-network boundaries.
- `competency-modeling` — translation of KSAOs/capabilities into strategic HR language and assessment systems.
- `ai-workforce-impact-measurement` — the AI-era analogue: distinguishing human capability, AI capability, adoption/use, workflow design, and downstream performance.
- `job-analysis` — upstream definition of work, KSAOs, and valued outcomes that human-capital measures inherit.

## AI-era synthesis angle

When the user frames HCM relevance around AI capital or convergence of human and AI work, preserve that context without letting it overtake the source summary. Good synthesis language: HCR measurement supplies a cautionary measurement analogue for future human-AI capability indices. If human capability is already measured with deficient or contaminated indicators, adding AI capability can compound ambiguity unless the unit of analysis and construct boundaries are explicit.
