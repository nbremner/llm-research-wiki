# Cognitive surrender topic assessment

Use this pattern when ingesting sources about AI answer adoption, uncritical acceptance of AI reasoning, System 3 / external cognition, or users following confidently wrong AI outputs.

## Central distinction

Do not flatten these sources into generic `automation-complacency` or broad `human-ai-collaboration` when the focal construct is the moment an AI-generated answer becomes the user's own judgment with minimal scrutiny. Treat `cognitive-surrender` as a process-level topic: narrower than complacency, more behavioral than trust, and distinct from productive cognitive offloading.

## Topic-map guidance

- **Create/update `cognitive-surrender`** when the source directly studies adoption of AI outputs, faulty-advice following, answer acceptance, or bypassing internal deliberation/verification.
- **Update `automation-complacency`** only for the broader vigilance/effort/oversight mechanism; make cognitive surrender a specific micro-mechanism rather than letting the broad page absorb the construct.
- **Update `critical-thinking`** when the source shows whether verification, explanation, judgment, or stewardship is bypassed versus relocated.
- **Update `human-ai-collaboration`** when the source shows that nominal human choice/control does not guarantee meaningful human judgment; collaboration can remain formally human-led while reasoning authority shifts to the AI output.
- **Consider `ai-mediated-learning`** if repeated surrender bypasses error detection, explanation, struggle, or transfer-building practice.

## Evidence framing

Good source-record claims preserve the difference between immediate task performance and judgment quality: surrender can improve performance when AI is correct and harm it when AI is wrong. Do not write as if all AI reliance is bad; write the calibration problem precisely.

## Limitation language

For lab reasoning studies with manipulated AI accuracy, note that they are strong for isolating the behavioral signature of surrender but do not by themselves prove the same pattern in live workplace tasks with expertise, accountability, collaboration, and independent verification channels.
