# AI-mediated organizational networks topic-assessment pattern

Use when ingesting sources whose central contribution is that AI changes human-human collaboration, knowledge-sharing, advice, or social-network structure inside organizations.

## When to create/promote this topic

Promote or update `[[ai-mediated-organizational-networks]]` when the source directly measures or theorizes changes in the organizational interaction graph: collaboration ties, knowledge-sharing ties, centrality, brokerage, expertise access, communication flow, coordination load, or cross-boundary knowledge exchange after AI enters work.

Do **not** flatten these sources into `[[ai-mediated-teamwork]]` by default. Teamwork pages cover bounded teams and team functions; organizational-network sources cover emergent ties across people, roles, groups, and knowledge domains. Do **not** flatten them into `[[human-ai-collaboration]]` either: the focal object is often the downstream human-human network changed by a human-AI interface, not only the user-tool relationship.

## Good synthesis moves

- Treat network rewiring as a work-design and workforce-impact mechanism, not merely an adoption outcome.
- Preserve the distinction between increased ties/centrality and improved coordination. More connectivity can mean better knowledge flow, but it can also mean overload, interruption, coordination fatigue, or concentration of requests on specialists.
- Track role-differentiated mechanisms: specialists may become more sought-after knowledge sources; generalists may use AI to improve coordination or throughput.
- Connect to `[[work-redesign]]`, `[[ai-mediated-work-experience]]`, `[[ai-mediated-teamwork]]`, and `[[ai-workforce-impact-measurement]]` when the evidence supports those links.

## Evidence cautions

- Self-reported network measures are useful but should be named as such; prefer digital-trace, multi-organization, or longer-window evidence before generalizing.
- Short-run network centrality should not be treated as durable social structure without persistence evidence.
- Role comparisons are not causal unless roles were randomly assigned or otherwise identified.

## Seed source

`[[2026-buechsenschuss-genai-organizational-networks]]` is the seed case: a randomized field experiment where grounded GenAI increased collaboration and knowledge-sharing centrality in one technology-services firm over three months, with different effects for specialists and generalists.
