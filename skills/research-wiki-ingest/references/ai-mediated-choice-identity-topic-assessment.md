# AI-mediated choice and identity topic assessment

Use this reference when ingesting sources about AI systems that recommend, filter, or choose on behalf of people in preference-relevant domains.

## Promote/create signal

Create or update `ai-mediated-choice-and-identity` when the source's central claim is that AI-mediated recommendation, personalization, or agentic choice changes:

- preference distinctiveness or homogenization across people;
- within-person choice diversity, exploration, or portfolio breadth;
- identity expression, self-definition, taste, agency, or behavioral variety;
- preference formation under delegated AI choice rather than only output quality or task performance.

Do not flatten these sources into `agentic-delegation`, `ai-mediated-work-experience`, `ai-receptivity`, or `human-ai-collaboration` unless the source is only incidentally about identity-relevant choice. The construct is distinct because an AI agent can select plausible options from inside a person's revealed preferences while still narrowing the person's enacted variety over time.

## Boundary with adjacent topics

- `agentic-delegation`: use for authority/execution handoff. Link to `ai-mediated-choice-and-identity` when the delegated act is preference-forming or identity-relevant.
- `ai-mediated-work-experience`: use for autonomy, competence, workload, identity, meaning, stress, and felt job quality in work. Link when delegated choices plausibly affect role identity, exploration, or ownership.
- `ai-receptivity`: use for willingness/preferences to use AI. Do not treat acceptance of personalized convenience as evidence that identity effects are harmless.
- `human-ai-task-allocation`: use when the design question is whether a human or AI should act on a specific task turn. Link when the allocation decision depends on whether the choice should remain human because it is identity-relevant or preference-forming.

## Example pattern

`2025-matz-basic-effect` promoted the topic because its core contribution was not generic delegation performance. It showed that generic and personalized GPT-4o agents choosing between users' real Facebook preferences reduced interpersonal distinctiveness, and that personalization preserved some between-person uniqueness while compressing within-person topical/psychological diversity. That makes personalization a trade-off, not an automatic autonomy-preserving solution.

## Synthesis cautions

- Treat non-work preference studies as mechanism evidence for I-O/work topics, not direct workplace field evidence.
- Preserve the distinction between agent choices, user uptake, and long-run identity effects; a study may observe AI choices without proving humans would accept them in live settings.
- Surface the personalization trade-off explicitly when present: personalization may reduce population-level homogenization while narrowing exploration inside the person's own preference space.
