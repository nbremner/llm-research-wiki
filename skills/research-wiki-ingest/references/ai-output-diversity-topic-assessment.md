# AI output diversity topic-assessment pattern

Use this reference when ingesting sources whose central contribution is open-ended AI output homogeneity, mode collapse, pluralism, diversity of generated options, individual preference fit, or evaluation failure on idiosyncratic human preferences.

## Create/update guidance

- **Create or update `ai-output-diversity`** when the source directly studies whether AI systems preserve meaningful variation in open-ended outputs, options, frames, metaphors, hypotheses, language, or judgments. This should not be flattened into generic `human-ai-collaboration`, `critical-thinking`, or `ai-supported-knowledge-synthesis` if the source's distinctive construct is diversity/pluralism rather than accuracy or productivity.
- **Update `ai-supported-knowledge-synthesis`** only when the source has a clear implication for synthesis work: narrowing of frames, hypotheses, interpretations, or evidence navigation under AI assistance.
- **Update `ai-mediated-choice-and-identity`** when the source links output homogeneity to preference formation, self-expression, identity-relevant choices, exploration, or repeated exposure to a narrow menu of acceptable options.
- **Update `construct-validity`** when the source shows that common evaluation labels such as quality, helpfulness, alignment, or preference fit are construct-deficient for open-ended tasks because they miss diversity, pluralism, minority preference, or individual-level fit.
- **Defer workplace-specific claims** unless the source directly studies work settings. For benchmark or model-evaluation papers, state the workforce implication as a mechanism or design concern, not as evidence that teams or workers have already become more homogeneous.

## Pitfalls

- Do not treat homogeneity as automatically bad. Some tasks legitimately benefit from standardization, consistency, and reduced variance. The useful synthesis question is where diversity is part of task quality rather than noise.
- Do not confuse diversity of outputs with demographic/cultural pluralism unless the source specifically measures those dimensions. Open-ended diversity can include divergent frames, metaphors, hypotheses, linguistic style, preference fit, or idea variety without mapping cleanly to identity groups.
- Do not let benchmark evidence become stronger than it is. If the source studies language-model outputs, say that directly; workplace implications require later field evidence.
