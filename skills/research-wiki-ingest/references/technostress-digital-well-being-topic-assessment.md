# Technostress / digital well-being topic assessment

Use this pattern when ingesting systematic reviews or empirical sources whose central contribution is technostress, digital strain, ICT stressors, digital well-being, workplace telepressure, techno-overload, techno-invasion, digital fatigue, technosuffering, or AI-specific stress/occupational-health effects.

## Topic-map default

Prefer promoting or updating `[[technostress-and-digital-well-being]]` when the source's central construct is digital-work strain or technology-mediated occupational well-being. Do not flatten these sources only into `[[ai-mediated-work-experience]]`, `[[cognitive-load-in-ai-assisted-work]]`, or `[[work-redesign]]`; those pages are adjacent, but technostress is a reusable occupational-health construct with its own nomological network.

## Create / update logic

- **Create now** when the source gives broad construct coverage, measurement framing, or systematic evidence on technostress/digital well-being rather than a narrow one-off stress result.
- **Update existing** `technostress-and-digital-well-being` when the source adds distinct evidence about stressor dimensions, buffers, cross-cultural/sector variation, occupational-health framing, or intervention design.
- **Also update** `ai-mediated-work-experience` only when the source speaks to felt job quality, burnout, recovery, autonomy, competence, identity, or well-being as part of the lived work experience.
- **Also update** `cognitive-load-in-ai-assisted-work` only when the source speaks to overload, digital fatigue, information sprawl, coordination burden, concentration loss, or mental resource depletion.
- **Also update** `work-redesign` only when the source yields design implications about platform simplification, communication protocols, control rights, support systems, autonomy, boundary management, or occupational-health governance.
- **Defer** narrow constructs like digital boundary management, platform simplification, or telepressure if they appear as submechanisms without enough independent evidence for their own page.

## Prose stance

Write against the flat claim that "technology causes stress." Distinguish specific stressor profiles: overload, invasion, complexity, insecurity, uncertainty, unreliable tools, communication pressure, platform proliferation, poor recovery boundaries, and AI control. Surface the possibility that some digital demands become challenge demands/techno-eustress under competence, autonomy, support, or control.

## Evidence cautions

Systematic reviews in this area often synthesize heterogeneous designs without meta-analysis. Treat them as construct maps and evidence aggregators, not pooled causal estimates. Watch for uneven geography, sector concentration, English/indexed-publication limits, grey-literature exclusion, and scarce longitudinal/intervention evidence.
