# Responsible AI in I-O psychology topic assessment

Use this pattern when ingesting sources whose central contribution is professional ethics, governance, validation responsibility, or practitioner role obligations around worker-facing AI systems in I-O psychology.

## Create / update pattern

Create or update `[[responsible-ai-in-io-psychology]]` when the source is primarily about:

- I-O psychologists' ethical responsibility to engage with AI decisions rather than remaining neutral observers.
- Worker-facing AI systems in hiring, assessment, training, monitoring, feedback, workforce relations, DEI, or replacement.
- Professional ethics frameworks such as APA principles, SIOP guidance, AI audit frameworks, or domain-specific validation standards.
- Balancing organizational efficiency, technical capability, and owner interests against worker dignity, privacy, autonomy, fairness, well-being, and accountability.
- The evidence threshold for deploying fast-moving AI systems before complete peer-reviewed validation evidence exists.

Do **not** flatten these sources into `[[algorithmic-assessment]]`, `[[automation-and-substitution]]`, or `[[ai-readiness]]` just because they mention selection tools, worker replacement, or organizational adoption. The focal construct is the professional ethics/governance role: who is responsible for asking what questions before worker-facing AI is deployed.

## Typical existing-topic feeds

- `[[algorithmic-assessment]]` when the source discusses AI hiring, scoring, interview delivery, validity, fairness, disclosure, applicant experience, or audit standards.
- `[[ai-mediated-learning]]` when the source discusses training chatbots, AI coaches, on-the-job advisers, or accountability for bad AI guidance.
- `[[automation-and-substitution]]` when the source discusses worker replacement, reskilling, job displacement, or capability/cost claims that need an ethics boundary.
- `[[evidence-based-management]]` when the source distinguishes efficiency, validity, compliance, fairness, usability, trust, and causal impact as different evidence claims.
- `[[ai-mediated-work-experience]]` when the source discusses monitoring, nudging, privacy, autonomy, psychological safety, or felt control.
- `[[ai-readiness]]` when the source implies readiness includes governance and professional standards, not only technical infrastructure.

## Completion-note cue

For these sources, the topic-map assessment should explicitly say whether `responsible-ai-in-io-psychology` is being promoted or updated, and why the source's central contribution is not merely adoption/readiness/compliance. The owner cares about avoiding over-consolidation; make the construct boundary visible.
