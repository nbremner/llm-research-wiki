# Threshold-based human learning topic assessment

Use this reference when ingesting sources that compare human learning with prediction/optimization models, especially sources about satisficing, threshold learning, convention formation, social learning, rule acquisition, sparse evidence, or human-vs-LLM learning differences.

## Reusable topic-map pattern

Do not flatten these sources automatically into `ai-mediated-learning`, `ai-literacy`, `cognitive-load-in-ai-assisted-work`, or `organizational-learning-capability` just because they mention learning or LLMs.

- `ai-mediated-learning` is the focal home when the source helps explain whether AI-supported work episodes build durable competence or bypass the practice, explanation, error-resolution, recall, and transfer operations that learning requires.
- `organizational-learning-capability` is the focal home when the source helps explain how individual learning, social exposure, peer evidence, or convention formation becomes stable collective routine.
- `ai-induced-skill-erosion` should be updated only when the source gives evidence about capability loss, weakened practice, or a plausible erosion precursor over repeated use; a general human-learning mechanism is not enough.
- `cognitive-load-in-ai-assisted-work` should be updated only when the source directly separates productive effort from extraneous burden or measures mental load.
- A new `threshold-based-human-learning` or `convention-learning` topic should be proposed only after multiple sources make threshold/satisficing learning a recurring design construct for AI training, workplace skill acquisition, norm formation, or organizational change.

## Good synthesis move

Preserve the user's framing when they flag the source as relevant because it distinguishes how humans and LLMs learn. The durable claim is usually not "LLMs are different from people" in the abstract. It is:

> Human skill acquisition may depend on sparse, structured, threshold-triggering evidence and transfer practice, so AI-mediated learning designs should not assume that more examples, more generated outputs, or better prediction automatically produce competence.

For adjacent non-AI sources, treat the paper as a boundary condition or mechanism source rather than overclaiming direct workplace-AI evidence.

## Example anchor

`2026-guilbeault-simple-threshold` updated `ai-mediated-learning` because it showed human convention and nonlinguistic pattern learning was better modeled as threshold-based satisficing than imitation or optimization, and it explicitly contrasted human learning with LLM-style data-intensive prediction. It also updated `organizational-learning-capability` because threshold/convention dynamics help explain how local evidence and peer exposure can stabilize into shared practice. The source was placed on the overview watchlist as `threshold-based-human-learning / convention learning` rather than promoted into a new topic from one source.
