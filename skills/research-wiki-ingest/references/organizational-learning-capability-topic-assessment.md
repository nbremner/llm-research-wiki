# Organizational learning capability topic assessment

Use this pattern when ingesting sources about organizational learning, learning organizations, learning capability, survey-feedback learning diagnostics, knowledge transfer, experimentation, or the organizational routines that convert local experimentation into changed practice.

## Topic-map decision

Promote or update `[[organizational-learning-capability]]` when the source's central contribution is about the organization's capacity to learn from experience, experimentation, failures, external knowledge, or local problem solving and integrate those lessons into routines. Do not flatten this into `[[ai-readiness]]` or `[[ai-adoption]]` just because the applied motivation is AI transformation.

The key distinction:

- `[[ai-readiness]]` asks whether an organization is prepared for a specific AI adoption purpose.
- `[[ai-adoption]]` asks what binds durable AI adoption once tools are available.
- `[[organizational-learning-capability]]` asks whether the organization can convert experimentation and local learning into shared knowledge, changed routines, and redesigned work.

For AI transformation, organizational learning capability is often the pre-AI construct that explains why some pilots compound into new ways of working while others remain disconnected anecdotes.

## Synthesis moves

- Frame organizational learning capability as the set of conditions that lets an organization turn experience, experimentation, failures, external knowledge, and local problem solving into changed behavior.
- For AI relevance, make the claim precise: AI experimentation becomes transformation only when lessons are surfaced, compared, transferred across teams, and embedded into routines/workflows.
- Keep measurement boundaries clear. Learning-capability instruments may diagnose enabling conditions; they are not direct evidence of knowledge creation, AI-use quality, work redesign, or performance improvement unless longitudinal/outcome evidence is present.
- Surface the governance tension: AI learning needs freedom to experiment and discuss failure, while responsible AI deployment needs controls, auditability, and risk limits.

## Useful validation cautions

For the Goh & Richards OLS case specifically:

- OLS dimensions: clarity of purpose/mission, leadership commitment/empowerment, experimentation/rewards, transfer of knowledge, teamwork/group problem solving.
- Early evidence included scale reduction, internal consistency, test-retest stability, organizational known-groups differences, positive correlation with job satisfaction, and negative correlation with formalization.
- Limitations: aggregate self-report, cross-sectional organizational comparisons, no direct performance or intervention-outcome test, normative learning-organization model acknowledged by the authors.
