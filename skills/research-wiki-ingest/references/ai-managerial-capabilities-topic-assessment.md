# AI managerial capabilities topic assessment

Use this reference when ingesting sources where the central contribution is managerial capability, leadership behavior, or knowledge-process infrastructure for AI exploitation/adoption — especially when the source sits between AI adoption, AI readiness, and organizational knowledge flow.

Session pattern: Hautamäki & Heikinheimo (2025), *Fully leveraging AI in B2B sales: Exploring sales managers’ capabilities and organizational knowledge processes*.

## Source shape

Typical signals:

- AI adoption/exploitation is framed as a managerial capability problem, not just tool access, employee willingness, or task fit.
- The source links managers’ data literacy/context understanding, social capital/knowledge-sharing routines, and change mindset to AI use.
- Evidence describes multilevel knowledge processes: individual knowledge creation, team knowledge sharing/transfer, and organizational knowledge-asset reconfiguration.
- The empirical setting may be domain-specific, such as B2B sales, and may rely on supervisor/top-manager accounts rather than observed behavior or performance outcomes.

## Topic-map default

Do **not** immediately create a new topic from a single managerial-capabilities source unless the managerial construct is the paper’s central reusable contribution *and* existing pages would distort it. Usually:

- **Update `ai-adoption`** when the source says adoption depends on managerial capabilities, use-case sharing, data/context sensemaking, experimentation routines, or conversion of tool access into shared practice.
- **Update `ai-readiness`** when the source says readiness includes local manager capability, cross-functional knowledge processes, data literacy, and the ability to translate AI into domain-specific work routines.
- **Update `ai-mediated-organizational-networks`** when the source gives mechanisms for cross-functional knowledge transfer, data as common language, or AI use changing who collaborates with whom. Keep this bounded if the source does not measure actual networks.
- **Defer/watchlist** a candidate such as `ai-managerial-capabilities / AI exploitation management` until multiple sources establish managerial capability as a recurring mechanism beyond one domain.

## Synthesis cautions

- Do not treat qualitative managerial-capability frameworks as evidence that AI adoption improved, sales performance increased, or network centrality changed unless those outcomes were directly measured.
- Preserve level of analysis: enterprise readiness can coexist with weak local manager capability; observed individual tool use can coexist with weak team/organizational knowledge transfer.
- If the source is industry-specific, name that boundary in topic prose and source limitations.
- If the evidence comes from supervisors/top managers rather than frontline managers/workers, say so. This is often a perception/design-theory source, not direct enacted-behavior evidence.

## Useful phrasing pattern

- Adoption: “adds a managerial-capability version of adoption” / “extends the map from finding use cases to building the managerial and knowledge-process capabilities that turn use cases into shared practice.”
- Readiness: “adds a managerial-capability layer to readiness” / “readiness includes whether middle and frontline managers can translate AI into domain-specific knowledge processes.”
- Networks: “qualitative evidence on the managerial work behind AI-mediated knowledge flow” / “a plausible management mechanism, not network measurement evidence.”
