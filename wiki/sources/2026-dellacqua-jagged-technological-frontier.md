# Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier: Field Experimental Evidence of the Effects of Artificial Intelligence on Knowledge Worker Productivity and Quality

**Citation.** Dell'Acqua, F., McFowland III, E., Mollick, E., Lifshitz, H., Kellogg, K. C., Rajendran, S., Krayer, L., Candelon, F., & Lakhani, K. R. (2026). *Navigating the jagged technological frontier: Field experimental evidence of the effects of artificial intelligence on knowledge worker productivity and quality.* Organization Science, Articles in Advance. https://doi.org/10.1287/orsc.2025.21838

**Summary.** A preregistered randomized field experiment with 758 Boston Consulting Group consultants tested GPT-4 assistance on realistic management consulting tasks. The paper introduces the "jagged technological frontier": AI improves performance on some knowledge-work tasks but worsens it on others that appear similarly difficult to humans. Inside the frontier, AI users completed more tasks, worked faster, and produced higher-quality outputs; outside it, AI users were less likely to solve the task correctly. The design implication is that AI adoption depends on task-level capability mapping and calibrated human judgment, not tool access alone.

## Key claims
- On 18 tasks selected to be inside the AI capability frontier, consultants using AI completed **12.2% more tasks** and completed them **25.1% more quickly** on average than consultants without AI.
- Inside-the-frontier AI use also improved output quality; the authors report significant positive treatment effects on expert-rated performance across creative, analytical, writing, and persuasiveness dimensions.
- On a complex managerial task selected to be outside the frontier, consultants using AI were **19 percentage points less likely** to produce correct solutions than consultants without AI.
- AI assistance improved the subjective coherence of outside-the-frontier responses even when correctness fell, showing how fluent AI-supported reasoning can make wrong answers look more persuasive.
- The same professional domain can contain both AI-augmentable and AI-degrading tasks, making the practical adoption problem one of task diagnosis, workflow mapping, and calibrated human verification.

## Evidence & limitations
- **Design:** preregistered randomized laboratory-in-the-field experiment with 758 BCG consultants. Participants first completed baseline tasks, then were randomly assigned to no AI, GPT-4 access, or GPT-4 access plus a prompt-engineering overview. Tasks were realistic consulting exercises; AI interaction logs were captured through a GPT-4 platform.
- **Evidence strength:** strong causal design for short-run task performance among highly skilled consultants, with blind expert grading and quantitative productivity, quality, timing, and correctness outcomes.
- **Limitations:** conducted in one professional-services firm and one period of GPT-4 capability; frontier boundaries are task- and model-specific and may shift quickly; the results do not show long-run learning, deskilling, adoption durability, equilibrium labor demand, or organizational performance effects.
