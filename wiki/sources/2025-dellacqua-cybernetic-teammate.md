# The Cybernetic Teammate: A Field Experiment on Generative AI Reshaping Teamwork and Expertise

**Citation.** Dell'Acqua, F., Ayoubi, C., Lifshitz, H., Sadun, R., Mollick, E. R., Mollick, L., Han, Y., Goldman, J., Nair, H., Taub, S., & Lakhani, K. R. (2025). *The cybernetic teammate: A field experiment on generative AI reshaping teamwork and expertise.* Harvard Business School Working Paper 25-043 / SSRN. https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5207588

**Summary.** This preregistered field experiment randomly assigned 776 Procter & Gamble professionals working on real product-innovation challenges to work individually or in two-person teams, with or without generative AI. AI improved solution quality enough that individuals with AI matched the performance of teams without AI, while teams with AI did not significantly outperform AI-supported individuals. The paper argues that GenAI can partly reproduce three traditional benefits of teamwork: performance gains, cross-functional expertise integration, and positive social or motivational experience.

## Key claims
- Teams without AI improved solution quality by about **0.24 standard deviations** over individuals without AI, while individuals with AI improved by about **0.37 standard deviations** and teams with AI by about **0.39 standard deviations**.
- Individuals using AI performed at a level statistically comparable to teams without AI, suggesting that AI can substitute for some short-run collaborative functions in bounded knowledge-work tasks.
- Without AI, R&D professionals produced more technically oriented product ideas and Commercial professionals produced more commercially oriented ideas; with AI, both groups produced more balanced solutions, similar to the cross-functional balance normally supplied by teams.
- AI-supported participants reported more positive emotions and fewer negative emotions after the task; individuals with AI showed a **0.457 standard deviation** increase in positive emotions, teams with AI showed a **0.635 standard deviation** increase, and both AI conditions reduced negative emotions by roughly **0.23 standard deviations**.
- The authors interpret AI as a boundary-spanning teammate rather than only a productivity tool: it supplies perspectives, language, and motivational cues that can change how expertise and social connection are organized in collaborative work.

## Evidence & limitations
- **Design:** preregistered randomized field experiment with 776 P&G professionals, crossing individual versus two-person team work with no-AI versus AI access on new product development challenges.
- **Evidence strength:** strong causal evidence for short-run effects on expert-rated product-innovation outputs, functional-balance measures, and self-reported emotions inside a real organization and real business domain.
- **Limitations:** working-paper evidence from one company, one task family, and one short experimental window; it does not establish long-run learning, team development, implementation quality, equilibrium staffing effects, or whether AI-supported individuals can replace teams when accountability, coordination, conflict, or execution are more complex.
- **Boundary note:** the PDF is an HBS working paper version; Crossref/SSRN metadata provides a public DOI, but the Drive PDF itself was treated as untrusted source text and used only for evidence extraction.
