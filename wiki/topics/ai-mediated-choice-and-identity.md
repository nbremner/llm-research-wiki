# AI-mediated choice and identity

AI-mediated choice and identity tracks how AI systems shape the preferences, choices, exploration patterns, and self-defining behaviors people enact when AI recommends, filters, or acts on their behalf. The construct is adjacent to work but not identical to work experience: the risk is that an agent can preserve apparent preference satisfaction while changing the variety and distinctiveness of what a person actually chooses over time.

[[2025-matz-basic-effect]] gives the wiki its first direct behavioral evidence. In a study of 110,000 real choices from 1,000 U.S. Facebook users, both generic and personalized GPT-4o agents selected less distinctive pages than a human baseline drawn from users' own followed pages. The design matters because the agents were not choosing alien options; they were selecting between things the person had already liked, so the flattening mechanism can operate inside the person's existing preference space.

The most useful contribution is the distinctiveness-diversity trade-off. Generic agents exerted the larger pull toward population-popular options, while personalized agents better preserved between-person distinctiveness but more strongly compressed within-person topical and psychological diversity. Personalization therefore does not simply solve agency loss; it may optimize toward the person's most typical self and reduce exploration of less central but still real preferences.

For I-O purposes, this topic is a warning against treating agentic delegation as a neutral convenience layer. If AI agents increasingly schedule, purchase, communicate, triage, or choose on behalf of workers, then identity, agency, and behavioral variety become design outcomes alongside efficiency and accuracy.

## Connections
- Relates to [[agentic-delegation]] because the effect appears when an AI system chooses on a person's behalf rather than merely advising them.
- Relates to [[ai-mediated-work-experience]] because identity, autonomy, exploration, and felt ownership are part of how delegated AI can change the experience of a role or workflow.
- Relates to [[ai-receptivity]] because people may welcome personalized convenience even when the downstream behavioral pattern narrows.
- Relates to [[human-ai-task-allocation]] because deciding whether the human or agent should choose may depend on whether the choice is identity-relevant, preference-forming, or worth preserving as human exploration.

## Contradictions & open questions
- [[2025-matz-basic-effect]] shows agent choices over Facebook page preferences, not workplace task choices. The source is therefore better evidence for a mechanism of preference flattening than for direct workplace effects.
- The study does not observe whether users would accept the agent-selected choices in live life or work settings. Actual identity effects depend on uptake, trust, and repeated delegation.
- Personalization appears protective for interpersonal distinctiveness but harmful for intrapersonal diversity. That trade-off needs to be separated from broad claims that personalization is either autonomy-preserving or manipulative.
