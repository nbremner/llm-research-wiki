# Research Map / Overview Template

Use this template for the public research-wiki's living overview layer. The live Notion page should be updated when the wiki's research questions, theme structure, frontier map, or known gaps materially change.

## Purpose

This wiki supports research and synthesis at the intersection of **AI workforce transformation** and **industrial-organizational psychology**.

It should be general-purpose enough to connect across constructs, methods, cases, and implementation domains, while still supporting deep dives on specific research questions when needed.

The Research Map is not a source database and not a private planning note. It is a public-source-backed orientation layer: what the wiki is trying to understand, how the concepts connect, where evidence is strong or thin, and which questions deserve deeper review.

## Scope

### In scope

- AI adoption and implementation in organizations
- Work redesign, job design, task allocation, and role re-architecting
- Human-AI collaboration, automation, augmentation, and substitution
- Skills, capabilities, learning, training, and workforce planning
- People analytics, measurement, psychometrics, and evaluation
- Leadership, employee attitudes, motivation, trust, commitment, and change
- Organizational governance, risk, ethics, and policy around workplace AI
- Productivity, performance, quality of work, and worker well-being outcomes
- Public case studies and public research on workforce transformation

### Out of scope

- Confidential work-derived examples, priorities, or synthesis
- Private notes, client/internal documents, or non-public artifacts
- Generic AI news with no workforce, organizational, or psychological relevance
- Pure technical ML content unless it materially affects workforce transformation, job design, assessment, analytics, or organizational practice

## Core lenses

Use these lenses to connect sources and concepts across the wiki.

1. **Technology-to-work translation** — What does a model/system/tool actually change about tasks, roles, coordination, skill demands, or decision rights?
2. **I-O construct validity** — Which psychological or organizational constructs are being invoked, measured, distorted, or ignored?
3. **Job and role architecture** — How are tasks aggregated into roles, and how do AI systems reconfigure role boundaries?
4. **Adoption and change** — What explains uptake, resistance, trust, misuse, adaptation, and sustained implementation?
5. **Capability formation** — What new skills, literacies, and learning systems are required?
6. **Measurement and evaluation** — How do we know whether AI workforce transformation is working, for whom, and at what cost?
7. **Governance and risk** — What should be constrained, audited, disclosed, or redesigned to protect fairness, agency, privacy, and quality of work?
8. **Strategic workforce transformation** — What changes at the level of workforce planning, organization design, labor substitution/augmentation, and value creation?

## Active research questions

Use this section to track durable questions that should drive ingest, review, and synthesis.

### Broad synthesis questions

1. How should organizations decide which tasks are candidates for automation, augmentation, delegation to agents, or human protection?
2. How does AI change job design, role boundaries, and task interdependence?
3. What I-O constructs are most useful for explaining successful AI adoption at work?
4. What does good measurement look like for AI workforce transformation beyond productivity anecdotes?
5. How do AI systems affect worker autonomy, competence, relatedness, motivation, trust, and commitment?
6. What governance practices reduce harm without freezing useful experimentation?
7. How should workforce planning adapt when skills, tasks, and role architectures are changing quickly?

## Update rules

Update this Research Map when:

- a review or gap map changes the understanding of a major topic;
- a high-value source introduces, sharpens, or contradicts a frontier question;
- repeated Inbox/Source items reveal a recurring theme not captured by the current map;
- a Concept subtype or taxonomy pressure becomes visible;
- a durable user query produces synthesis worth preserving.

Do not update this Research Map with:

- confidential or work-derived synthesis;
- casual one-off observations;
- unverified claims from weak sources;
- private priorities or plans that are not public-source-backed or owner-approved as research direction.

## Maintenance cadence

- Review after each major literature-review run.
- Review during monthly/periodic graph linting.
- Reconcile with Schema and Agent Operating Guide whenever taxonomy or role-boundary changes are proposed.
